# Chunk 03 — Lipolysis, Mobilization & Adrenaline

[3:07] Fat burning has two parts:
1. **Fat mobilization**
2. **Fat oxidation / utilization** (lipolysis)

[3:33] Stored fat = fatty acids + glycerol. Mobilization breaks the backbone (lipase) so fatty acids enter bloodstream.

[4:04] Inside cells, fatty acids must be **oxidized in mitochondria** → ATP.

[4:50] Main neuronal signal: **epinephrine (adrenaline)** — favors conversion of fatty acids to ATP in mitochondria.

[5:46] **Critical:** The adrenaline that stimulates fat oxidation is from **neurons that connect directly to fat** — a **local process**, not mainly circulating adrenal adrenaline.

[6:12] Specific movement patterns and environments that stimulate these neurons are a **powerful lever** for fat loss.
