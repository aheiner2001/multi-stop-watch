      Fidget Prompts NEAT · epinephrine → fat mobilization

      Staccato movements—knee bounce, pacing, standing breaks—trigger epinephrine from neurons that connect directly to fat cells, stimulating mobilization and oxidation. This is the best entry point if traditional exercise feels like a barrier.

      NEAT Step Tracker Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis

      Research by Rothwell & Stock (and later 2015/2017 studies): people who fidget—knee bounce, pacing, standing—burn considerably more fat at the same food intake. On a steep deficit your nervous system subconsciously cuts movement; steps + fidgeting counter that plateau.

      Neuro Protocols

      Shiver Protocol 1–5× per week · succinate → brown fat

      Shivering—not just cold—releases succinate, activating brown-fat thermogenesis. The in/out pattern (not staying in continuously) keeps triggering shiver and avoids cold adaptation that blunts the effect.

      1. Enter cold water/shower (~60°F+ when starting) until shivering begins
      2. Exit for 1–3 minutes — do not towel off
      3. Re-enter and repeat for ~3 total in/out cycles
      Cold safety: Extreme cold can shock the heart. Start uncomfortable but safe (~60°F). Get physician clearance before cold protocols.
