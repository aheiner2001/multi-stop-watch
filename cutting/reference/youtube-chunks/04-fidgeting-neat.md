# Chunk 04 — Tool: Fidgeting & NEAT

[6:38] **Shivering** is a strong stimulus for epinephrine into fat → mobilization + oxidation.

[7:10] **Rothwell & Stock (1960s–70s):** People who overate without gaining weight engaged in lots of **subtle movement** — fidgeters.

[8:18] 2015 & 2017 studies: knee bounce, standing, pacing several times daily → considerable fat/weight loss **at same food intake**.

[8:43] For overweight people **averse to exercise**, fidgeting is a good **entry point**.

**Protocol — Fidget:**
- Staccato subtle movements: knee bounce, pacing, standing, walking around
- Triggers local epinephrine from fat-innervating neurons
- Can offset excessive meals or chronic overeating (won't fix chronic overeating alone)

**App:** NEAT page fidget checklist + step counter.
